Epilogue

        13 of the 20 players on the 1980 United States Olympic hockey team went on to play in the NHL.
        Ken Morrow joined the New York Islands, and helped the team win four straight Stanley Cup titles. After retiring, he became the director of scouting.
        Bill Baker followed his dream of becoming an oral and maxillofacial surgeon.
        Jack O’Callahan played for several NHL teams, before retiring and started to work in finance.
        Steve Janaszak also worked in finance.
        Jim Craig played hockey for 4 years, then founded the company Gold Medal Strategies. Mike Eruzione retired right after the games (Coffey 262). He later became a commentator for ABC, commentating NHL games and a few Olympic hockey games.
        Mike Ramsey played in the NHL and became an all-star 4 times. After retiring, he became the assistant coach of the Buffalo Sabers, then the Minnesota Wild.
        David Silk continued to play hockey, and then became the assistant coach at Boston University. He later became an investment banker.
        Neal Broten played hockey for 17 years before becoming a horse farm operator.
        Mark Johnson played in the NHL, then in Italy and Austria. He now coaches women’s hockey at University of Wisconsin, and was the head coach of the 2010 women’s hockey team who won the silver medal.
        Steve Christoff only played in the NHL for a few years before becoming a pilot of Endeavor Air.
        Mark Wells played in the minor leagues for 2 years. He auctioned his gold medal for $40,000, and it recently sold again for $300,000.
        Mark Pavelich would play for the New York Rangers, and after 12 years he became a land developer in Minnesota. He also auctioned off his gold medal, and it was bought for $262,900. He did this to give let his daughter be more comfortable financially.
        Eric Strobel only played hockey for a couple of years due to injuries. He would later become a telephone sales executive, then became co-owner of a golf club.
        Bob Suter played in the minor league for 2 years, then opened a sporting goods store. He died of a heart attack in 2014 at the age of 57.
        Dave Christian played in the NHL for 16 years before retiring and becoming a glass manufacturer.
        Buzz Schneider played hockey in Switzerland for 3 years, then became a real estate broker.
        Rob McClanahan also played for the New York Rangers, and retired after 4 years. He now works at ThinkEquity partners, where he is the managing director.
        Phil Verchota played hockey in Finland, and then joined the 1984 U.S. hockey team. He is now the senior vice president of First American Bank.
        John Harrington played in the minor leagues, then also joined the 1984 hockey team. He would later become the head coach of the Minnesota State University women’s hockey team.
        Head coach Herb Brooks coached the 1998 French hockey team and the 2002 USA hockey team. He died at age 66 in a car accident (Bono).
